**Negotiation leverage:** Given the below-market price already, the leverage here is less about further reducing rent and more about (a) getting written confirmation of square footage and unit condition before applying, and (b) using the day-one listing status and short 2D listing history as leverage to ask for flexibility on move-in date or a short courtesy hold while you tour, since the price suggests the landlord wants a fast lease-up.

## 8. Negotiation Recommendation

Given the unit is already priced **below** the neighborhood comp range (Section 2), there is limited room to negotiate the base rent further down — pushing too hard on price risk signaling desperation without justification, since $1,799 is already competitive. Instead, focus negotiation energy on:

- **Move-in date flexibility** — if you need a few extra weeks, ask, since the listing suggests the landlord may want a fast lease-up (which strengthens your position to ask for flexibility rather than pay a rent reduction).
- **Written confirmation of costs** — insist on a written breakdown of all move-in costs (first month, security deposit, application fee, any broker fee) before applying, given the ambiguity in the listing.
- **Minor repair/upgrade asks** — if a tour reveals cosmetic issues (e.g., no dishwasher, worn fixtures), ask if the landlord will address them (e.g., install a portable dishwasher, fresh paint) as a condition of signing, rather than asking for a straight rent cut.
- **Security deposit or first-month timing** — with rent already below market, a modest ask (e.g., a slightly reduced security deposit or split first-month payment) is more realistic than a rent reduction.

**Leverage:** The building's rapid-relisting history (2D → 4D in 3 days) suggests urgency on the landlord's side to keep units occupied, which is your primary point of leverage — not price, but terms and flexibility.

**Risks:** Pushing too hard on an already below-market unit could cause the landlord to simply move to the next applicant, especially if demand is otherwise healthy in this area.

**Draft message to the broker:**

> Hi Mikhail, thanks for sharing the listing for 1011 Ocean Avenue #4D. I'm very interested and would like to schedule a tour. Before I do, could you confirm the exact square footage, whether the listing photos are specific to this unit, the lease term being offered, and whether there's a broker fee I would be responsible for? I'd also like to know if there's any flexibility on the move-in date, as I may need [X weeks / a specific date]. Thank you!
